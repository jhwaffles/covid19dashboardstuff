COVID19
Exploratory Data Analysis and Visualization

Background

This page contains and assortment of plots and analysis using up-to-date COVID19 data in USA. It is meant to be a personal project to practice and showcase skills (python, pandas, plotly, matplotlib, and some basic html) on current data. Data is sourced from https://covidtracking.com. I use pandas module to clean up and arrange the dataframe and plotly/matplotlib modules to make the interactive graphs.

Graphs and Analysis

Current hospital load (Hospitalized and in ICU) for each state sorted by states with the highest number of cases.

Covid19 deaths per capita (per 1 million population) for each state. A 7 day moving average is applied to smooth out the fluctuations in reporting.

Trends here usually follow positive cases and hospitalized/ICU patients.

Positive test rate (green) and daily tests per capita (black line). We would like to see a decreasing positive test rate, signalling the percentage of potentially infectious is decreasing.

Hospitalizations are trending up for certain states and down for others. This bar chart shows the change in hospitalizations per capita (per 1 million population) in the last 14 days. States with a surge of hospitalizations in the past 14 days have the largest positive values.

An alternate view of the same data is shown in an interactive map form.

From July - Taking a look at New York individually I wanted to see how numbers looked per county. Data is sourced from https://github.com/nytimes/covid-19-data. Below is a visualization. Note: The data for 5 counties (Queens, Kings, Richmond, Bronx, and New York City) were lumped into one county of New York City.

I wanted to see what trends are present when looking at the number of deaths versus population density of individual counties. Data was graphed on a log-log basis due to orders of magnitude difference in the numbers for new york city compared to other counties.

There is a positive correlation for the # of deaths and the population density in NY counties. What about normalizing for deaths per 1M?
